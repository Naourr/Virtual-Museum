Spanish Period Dance

Filipino folk dances during the Spanish colonial period were shaped by a blend of indigenous traditions and Spanish influences, producing graceful and lively performances that reflected themes of courtship, community, and celebration. Dances like Cariñosa, Pandanggo sa Ilaw, and Kuratsa combined European musical styles with Filipino movement and expression, creating a unique cultural identity. These dances became an important part of fiestas and social life, preserving Filipino heritage while showcasing the lasting impact of Spanish colonization.

Featured Dances

Cariñosa

Artist: Filipino Folk Dancers
Year: 16th Century
Origin: Panay Island, Philippines

Cariñosa is a traditional Filipino dance that shows a romantic courtship between a man and a woman. Dancers use fans and handkerchiefs while performing soft and graceful movements. The dance expresses love, shyness, and affection between partners. Its name comes from the Spanish word cariñosa, meaning affectionate, showing the Spanish influence on Filipino culture.

Jota Moncadeña

Artist: Filipino Folk Dancers
Year: Early 20th Century
Origin: Moncada, Tarlac, Philippines

Jota Moncadeña is a lively Filipino folk dance influenced by the Spanish Jota. Dancers perform quick steps while clapping or sometimes using castanets. The dance is energetic and joyful, usually performed during celebrations. It reflects the strong Spanish influence on Filipino music and dance traditions.

Rigodon de Honor

Artist: Filipino Elite Society Dancers
Year: 1920
Origin: Philippines

Rigodon de Honor is a formal Filipino dance performed during elegant social gatherings and balls during the Spanish colonial period. It was influenced by European court dances brought by the Spanish. Couples dance in organized patterns with graceful movements. The dance represents the refined lifestyle of the Filipino elite during that time.

Pandanggo sa Ilaw

Artist: Filipino Folk Dancers
Year: 18th Century
Origin: Lubang Island, Mindoro, Philippines

Pandanggo sa Ilaw is a popular Filipino dance that involves balancing oil lamps on the head and hands. The dancers move gracefully while keeping the lamps steady. The dance is lively and rhythmic, often performed during festivals. It shows a mix of Spanish and Filipino cultural influences in its music and style.

Sayaw sa Bangko

Artist: Filipino Folk Dancers
Year: 1500s
Origin: Pangasinan, Philippines

Sayaw sa Bangko is a dance performed on top of a narrow wooden bench. Dancers must show balance, coordination, and careful footwork while performing steps on the bench. The dance is entertaining and skillful to watch. It reflects the joyful and festive spirit of Filipino communities.

Kuratsa

Artist: Filipino Folk Dancers
Year: 19th Century
Samar and Leyte, Philippines

Kuratsa is a lively courtship dance usually performed during festivals, weddings, and community celebrations. The dance shows playful interaction between a man and a woman. It features energetic movements and expressive gestures. Kuratsa combines traditional Filipino culture with Spanish musical influences.
