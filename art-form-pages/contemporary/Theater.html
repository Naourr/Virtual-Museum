Contemporary Theater

Contemporary Philippine theater isn't just about actors in costumes; it’s a mirror to the nation. It’s where our deep-seated love for the zarzuela and senakulo (traditional folk plays) collides with the gritty reality of modern-day Manila. Here, the stage isn't just a platform—it's a space for truth-telling.

Featured Literature

Ang Huling El Bimbo The Musical

Playwright / Director: Dingdong Novenario (Playwright) & Dexter M. Santos (Director)
Theatre Company: Full House Theater Company (FHTC)
Year Created: 2018
Medium: Musical Theater (Performing Arts)

Ang Huling El Bimbo The Musical takes the iconic songs of the Eraserheads, weaves a deeply emotional story of friendship, and creates a spectacular stage experience. The powerful play shows the innocent joy of college life, reveals the dark realities of the adult world, and highlights the painful consequences of unspoken truths. This production steps away from traditional Broadway imports, champions original Filipino storytelling, and connects perfectly with local audiences. By mixing energetic dance numbers, delivering heartbreaking ballads, and tackling heavy social issues like abuse and poverty, the creators celebrate 90s nostalgia, expose the tragic loss of innocence, and honor the enduring power of music. Ultimately, the musical invites the audience to remember their own past, to forgive their own mistakes, and to appreciate the brilliant legacy of Original Pilipino Music.

Rak of Aegis

Playwright / Director: Maribel Legarda
Musical Arrangement / Book by: Myke Solomon / Liza Magtoto
Theatre Company: Globe Live / Atlantis Theatrical Productions
Year Created: 2014
Origin: Philippines

“Rak of Aegis” is a contemporary Filipino musical that tells the story of a resilient community living in a flood-prone barangay, using the hit songs of the iconic band Aegis. It combines humor, drama, and high-energy musical numbers, making it relatable and entertaining for modern audiences. The musical highlights Filipino culture through local humor, social commentary, and references to everyday life, giving audiences both laughter and reflection. Its significance in the Philippines lies in how it innovatively blends popular music with theatre, showing that contemporary Philippine theatre can address social issues while remaining fun and culturally authentic. It has attracted young audiences and theatre enthusiasts alike, helping Filipino musicals gain recognition nationally and internationally as a vibrant form of contemporary art.

Liwanag sa dilim

Playwright / Director: Robbie Guevara
Musical arrangement: Book and direction by Robbie Guevara; musical arrangements and orchestration by Orlando Dela Cruz
Theater Company: 9 Works Theatrical
Year Created: 2025
Origin: Philippines

This Liwanag sa Dilim is an adaptation of Jose Rizal’s Noli Me Tangere that’s also reminiscent of the recent television adaptation Maria Clara at Ibarra, with a character purportedly from present day somehow transplanted to the fictional past during the novel’s timeline. This story follows Elesi (Anthony Rosaldo), a Gen Z man who is mourning the suspicious death of his father. He is handed an amulet of some kind which allows him to travel across time and space. With this newfound ability, Elesi embarks on a mission to uncover his past. This is how he ends up in the world of Noli which, if the story’s logic is to be followed, not fictional from his point of view, but historical past.

Buruguduystunstugudunstuy: Ang Parokya ni Edgar Musical

Playwright / Director: Dexter M. Santos
Musical Arrangement / Book by: Rody Vera (book)
Theatre Company: Newport World Resorts / Full House Theater Company
Year Created: 2023
Origin: Philippines

Buruguduystunstugudunstuy is a Filipino musical play that features songs by the band Parokya ni Edgar. It tells the story of four women who unexpectedly enter a strange and magical world. As they go on their journey, they face their personal problems and learn more about themselves. The musical combines humor, music, and fantasy elements. It shows themes of friendship, identity, and self-discovery.

A Game of Trolls

Playwright / Director:Liza Magtoto (Playwright) / Maribel Legarda (Director)
Theatre Company: PETA (Philippine Educational Theater Association)
Year Created: 2017
Origin: Philippines

The play follows Heck, a cynical young man who works in a "troll farm" manipulating social media narratives to revise history and spread disinformation. As he navigates this digital battlefield, he is haunted by the "ghosts" of the past who emerge from the internet cloud to challenge his apathy and reveal the human cost of his online actions. It serves as a powerful exploration of how digital transformation has been weaponized to alter public memory, forcing the audience to confront the reality of fake news in the age of algorithms.

Kung Paano Ako Naging Leading Lady

Playwright / Director: Carlo Vergara(Playwright) / Chris Martinez (Director)
Theatre Company: Dalanghita Productions Year Created: 2015
Origin: Philippines

Kung Paano Ako Naging Leading Lady is a Filipino tragicomedy musical play that describes the story of a domestic helper who struggles with poverty, family conflict, and her unfulfilled dreams. This play shows sibling rivalry, sacrifice, and the protagonist’s desire to achieve a better life and shows this through humor and emotional moments. While the protagonist faces various challenges, she discovers her own strength and later finds her place as the leading lady of her own story.
