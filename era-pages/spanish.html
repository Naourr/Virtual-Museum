Spanish

During the Spanish colonial era, Philippine art served primarily as a tool for religious conversion. This influence permeated every medium—from painting and literature to music and theater. While secular forms like courtship dances persisted, the cultural landscape was largely dominated by ecclesiastical themes, evidenced by the proliferation of grand churches, religious icons (santos), and liturgical art.
