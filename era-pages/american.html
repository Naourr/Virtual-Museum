American

The American Era of Philippine arts (1898–1946) marked a shift toward Western styles and themes due to U.S. colonization. Art became more focused on realism, formal education, and secular subjects, moving away from purely religious Spanish influences. This period saw the rise of academic art training, public education, and new media like painting, literature in English, and architecture, blending Filipino identity with American and European artistic traditions.
